How We Make Money

Last updated: July 12, 2026 · Effective date: July 12, 2026

Path to Insure is free for you to use — no subscription, no paywall. That's possible because we earn money in a few specific ways, all described below in plain language. This page is the fuller version of the short “How we make money” note you see near comparison results, calculators, and carrier reviews throughout the site.

1. The short version

We earn money mainly through affiliate commissions when you click a partner link or get connected with a carrier through our comparison tools, plus advertising shown on our educational content. We do not charge you anything to use the site, and we do not let those revenue relationships decide what we write or how we rank comparison results.

2. Affiliate commissions — our main revenue source

When you click certain links, or when you use a comparison tool or calculator and get connected with a carrier or partner, we may earn a commission or referral fee. This happens on comparison pages, calculator results, and carrier review pages — the places on the site designed to help you take the next step toward getting a quote.

This disclosure appears in the same place as the link or button it applies to, before you click it — not buried only on this page or in the footer. See our full Affiliate Disclosure for the details.

3. Lead referrals

In some categories — Medicare, small-business coverage, and long-term care are common examples — a self-serve click isn't always the natural next step, so instead we may pass along the details you enter into a calculator or comparison tool (like your ZIP code and coverage needs) to a carrier or agent partner so they can follow up with you directly. We only do this when you take an action that requests it — submitting a form, for example — and we're upfront about it at that point, not after the fact.

4. Advertising

We show display ads (served by a third-party ad network, such as Google AdSense or a comparable network) on our educational guide and glossary content — the pages meant purely to help you learn, not to compare or buy. We deliberately keep display ads off our comparison pages, calculator results, and carrier review pages, so they're never mixed up with the affiliate links that actually move you toward a quote. See our full Advertising Disclosure for how we label ads and any sponsored content.

5. Sponsored listings and content (if and when they appear)

A carrier may occasionally pay for enhanced visibility within a comparison table (for example, a “Featured Partner” badge), or we may publish an article paid for by a carrier or brand. Both are clearly labeled — “Sponsored,” “Featured Partner,” or “Paid Placement” — directly on the listing or at the top of the article, never disguised as ordinary editorial content.

6. Email

If you sign up for our newsletter, any partner link included in an email carries the same disclosure as it would on the site, plus the unsubscribe and sender-identification requirements federal law requires for commercial email.

7. What this doesn't affect

These revenue relationships do not affect:

  • The price you're quoted— clicking our link doesn't change what a carrier charges you for a policy.
  • Which carriers we cover editorially or how we describe them in guides and reviews.
  • How our comparison tools order or rank results — sponsored placement (§5 above) is always visibly labeled as such, separate from organic ranking logic.
  • What we publish — see our Editorial Policy for how we keep editorial decisions independent of monetization.

8. Questions

Want more detail on a specific partnership or revenue relationship? Email partnerships@pathtoinsure.com. See also our Affiliate Disclosure, Advertising Disclosure, and Editorial Policy.

How we make money: Path to Insure may earn a commission when you click a partner link or get a quote through one of our comparisons. This never changes what we cover or how we rank information, and it doesn't affect the price you pay. Read our full disclosure.
